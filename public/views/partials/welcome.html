Who is Jono?

Hi, my name is Jono! I am an urban planner, proud South High graduate, and a product of our parks. I do community-based engagement, planning, and research work for a wide array of public, private, and non-profit organizations in the Twin Cities. I am also an active community member! I am currently chair of the Lowry Hill East Neighborhood Open Spaces Committee. I spearheaded the development of one of Minneapolis' first parklets in the CARAG neighborhood. I have a degree in English from Pacific Lutheran University and a masters degree in Urban and Regional Planning from the Humphrey School of Public Affairs.

Why is Jono running for Park Board?

"I'm running for Park Board to advocate for a park system that works for everybody. Today and for generations to come."

I am running for Park Board to make our parks friendlier for everybody. I have the experience, the passion, and the ideas to be an effective, responsive steward of our beloved parks. As Park Board Commissioner for District 4, I am passionate about working on these key areas.

  • Parks for and by the people. Let’s empower local stewardship of our parks, by allowing more local, active participation in bettering our parks, moving towards participatory budgeting, and creating meaningful partnerships with community groups.
  • Safe parks are active parks. We should be spending more money on youth programming than we do on police enforcement, not the other way around.
  • Be bold stewards of our environment to combat climate change. Let’s invest in rain gardens, carbon sequestration, and end the use of pesticides in the parks.
  • Weave an equity framework through all park decisions. I am well-versed in performing equity and policy analyses. I wrote a transformative equity-based study on capital budget spending in Saint Paul that spurred an overhaul of the entire spending process. I will bring this perspective to evaluating all our park spending.
